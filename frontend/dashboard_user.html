Scrap Insights: India & Maharashtra

Facts, figures, and the complete journey from waste to wealth.

Advantages of Scrap Recycling

  • Conserves natural resources like timber, water, and minerals by replacing raw material extraction.
  • Saves up to 95% energy for metals like aluminium compared to mining new ore.
  • Significantly reduces greenhouse gas emissions and carbon footprint across supply chains.
  • Creates local jobs and circular economy — especially in Maharashtra's 3.5 MT/year industry.
  • Diverts waste from landfills, preventing groundwater and soil pollution.

Disadvantages to Address

  • Improper e-waste disposal causes soil and water contamination with toxic heavy metals.
  • Informal workers face health hazards with no protective equipment or safety standards.
  • Mixed/unsorted scrap requires costly manual separation and reduces recovery rates.
  • Long-distance transportation for processing adds cost and carbon emissions.
  • Fluctuating scrap prices make it unreliable income for small-scale collectors.
